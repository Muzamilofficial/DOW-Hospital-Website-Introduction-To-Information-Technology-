About Us

Founded in December 2003, the Dow University of Health Sciences (DUHS) has been developing a tradition of excellence in education, research and community service. DUHS has educated and trained more than 8,000 students and residents from twenty academic institutes. The University has nearly 3,000 employees, including approximately 850 faculty members at present.

The Dow University of Health Sciences (DUHS) offers a multitude of undergraduate and postgraduate programs that cater to the needs in healthcare sciences by providing quality human resources in those fields.



A Focus On Community Healthcare & Services
The Dow University of Health Sciences, since its inception, has been focusing on community services to provide preventive and diagnostic treatment along with rehabilitation facilities for community health care specially to the under privileged class. The students of various undergraduate and postgraduate programs of DUHS are also actively involved with the faculty members in all the Civic Engagements of DUHS.



The Pro-Vice Chancellors
The Vice-Chancellor works closely with the Pro-Vice-Chancellors, as in his absence, they are assigned the charge of the office of the Vice- Chancellor and serve as the Competent Authority. The Pro-Vice Chancellors are designated for each campus, and serve as strategic heads for Education, Research and Learning and Innovative activities of the University.



The Role of Pro-Vice Chancellors

The Pro-Vice Chancellors are the competent authority, in the absence of the Vice-Chancellor.

The responsibilities of the Pro-Vice Chancellors are to:


Set academic and corporate goals and objectives for the University.

Further the university’s interests by networking with academic and business organizations nationally and internationally.

Represent the university in external and internal committees

Oversee learning, teaching, research activities and the student experience across the university programs.
