Introduction

Haematology is the study of blood and blood disorders. These vary from anemia to diseases such as leukemia. While many of these patients are undergoing investigations and treatments for blood cancers, others have non-cancerous disorders. There are patients with some blood disorders who may require bone marrow transplantation for getting cure from their diseases.

Clinical Haematology and bone marrow transplantation is a newly establishing department at DUHS, Ojha campus. It is the first clinical haematology program in public sector with all the facilities available for the treatment of blood disease patients under one roof ranging from outpatient consultation up to bone marrow transplantation.


Out-Patient Services

Clinics
Separate adult and paediatric haematology clinics are providing the facility for the treatment of haematological disorders like anemia, thalassemia & haemoglobinopathies, thrombosis and haemostasis, bleeding disorders, inherited blood disorders, myeloma, myeloproliferative neoplasms, lymphomas, acute and chronic leukemias, bone marrow failure syndromes like aplastic anemia and for bone marrow transplant patients.


Phlebotomy Area
Considering the outpatients’ ease, a lab sample collection area is available adjacent to clinics.The issue for patients is that if a blood sample is poorly collected or destroyed during transportation, the results may be inaccurate and misleading to the clinician, or the patient may have to undergo the inconvenience of repeat testing.

Day Care Unit
It is designed for patients with blood diseases who doesn’t require long admissions and can be discharged on the same day. These include patients who require blood transfusion, intravenous drug administration, short duration chemotherapies, procedures like intrathecal chemotherapy & bone marrow aspiration & trephine etc.


Emergency Room
Considering Separate rooms and haematological emergencies are allocated on the same floor so that the health care should be provided to these critical and immune-compromised patients by the haematologists from the beginning avoiding routine delays which patients face in usual emergency rooms of hospitals.


Consultative Haematology
Those patients admitted in services other than haematology and require expert opinion on some co-existing blood problem are looked after by the haematologists who provide their advice to facilitate the management of these patient.


Dispatching Pharmacy
In dispatching area of pharmacy, our qualified pharmacists issue the medications to the patients after proper checks as a quality control measure.
