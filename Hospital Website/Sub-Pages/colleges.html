Dow Medical College (DMC)
History Overview



Dow Medical College (DMC) was founded in 1945, by converting the old medical school of Hyderabad, which was originally started by Dr. Homstead in 1881. Subsequently the College was shifted to function in old N.J.V. High School Building, Karachi, at the beginning of 1946. Dr. Hemandas R. Wadhwani, the Minister of Public Health and Medicine of Sindh Government took keen interest in the establishment of this institution along with Sir Hugh Dow, then Governor of Sindh, helped in establishing this college. The college was subsequently named after him.

Soon after, a new building of the College was constructed within the premises of the School and Civil Hospital was attached as the teaching hospital for the College, and was affiliated to the University of Bombay. After creation of Pakistan, the College was affiliated to Sindh University at Karachi in December, 1947. Subsequently in 1951, when Sindh University was shifted to Hyderabad and Karachi University came into existence, the College was affiliated with Karachi University. In December 2003, Dow University of Health Sciences was created as a University with three constituent institutions, one of which was Dow Medical College.

Since the founding of the College, many additions and improvements have taken place. Today, it is full-fledged institution imparting both undergraduate and postgraduate studies, and has all the departments manned by qualified and experienced staff. It is also one of the largest medical institutions in the country.

Dow International Medical College (DMC)
Introduction



he Dow International Medical College (DIMC) was established in 2007. DIMC is a constituent college of Dow University of Health Sciences, offered specifically to facilitate the medical education for the children of overseas Pakistanis residing all around the world. DIMC is following the semester system as per the directives of Pakistan Medical and Dental Council (PMDC). A spirally integrated modular curriculum has been adopted by Dow University of Health Sciences since 2009. DIMC is included in the WHO directory of Medical Schools and IMED - Foundation for Advancement of International Medical Education and Research.

The college resides within a state-of-the-art building, constructed over an area of 200,000 square feet. The college has five fully equipped laboratories, including a Power Lab, a-well established Anatomy and Forensic Museum, an exotic Dow Animal House harboring horses, rats and mice of different type and strains, rabbits, monkeys and snakes for research purposes, a skills lab for undergraduates and postgraduates, along with a Professional Development Centre for faculty development and training. The college has a three storied library with a diverse book selection, a digital library with three hundred work stations, a reading room and a video conference room.

DIMC currently has an affiliated teaching hospital at the Ojha Campus. The medical college has offered its clinical services through the Dow University Hospital, with a flourished OPD Block with busy clinics of medicine and surgery, along with their allied disciplines. Marvelous Diagnostic and Research Facilities of the college have gained great attention of Karachi city. These facilities were established by building the Dow Diagnostic Research and Reference Laboratories and the Radio Diagnostic Complex. The National Institute of Liver and Gastrointestinal Diseases and the National Institute of Diabetes and Endocrinology are not only providing medical education to students, but also providing commendable diagnostic and treatment services to the patients.

Beautifully built male and female hostels with a comfortable atmosphere and efficient security panels are available for the students of DIMC are also present within the campus. Extracurricular activities including indoor and outdoor games among the students are facilitated by a large cricket ground and a state of the art Gymnasium.

DOW INTERNATIONAL DENTAL COLLEGE
Introduction



The Dow International Dental College was established in 2011 at Ojha Campus and is a constituent college of Dow University of Health Sciences admitting 50 BDS students per year, specifically to facilitate the medical education for the children of overseas Pakistanis residing all around the world. Currently two batches have passed out and 200 undergraduate students are studying for the degree of Bachelors of Dental Surgery (BDS)

The college building is a state of art with fully equipped laboratories including the Power Lab, well established Oral Biology and Community Dentistry Museum, Ceramic laboratory, Oral Pathology and Pre-clinical laboratories. The college has modern library with book section and a digital library as well.

Dow International Dental Hospital has a flourished OPD Block equipped with 75 dental chairs with busy clinics of Oral Diagnosis, Periodontology, Operative Dentistry, Endodontic, Prosthodontics, Orthodontics and Oral and Maxillo-Facial Surgery. Marvelous Diagnostic and Research Facilities of the college has gained a great attention of the Karachi city

Extracurricular activities including outdoor and indoor games among the students are also facilitated in the campus.

DOW COLLEGE of PHARMACY
Introduction



Dow College of Pharmacy, DUHS, is an endeavor to bring into existence an academy that is capable of catering the entire aspects of health profession. Pharmacy is an integral part of Health care system. Pharmacy professionals ensure safe and effective use of drugs throughout the society whether in hospitals, clinics or community. Pharmacy aims to enhance public awareness for the appropriate use of medicinal substances and discourage misuse. Similarly, Pharmacists also play vital roles in pharmaceutical industry to look after production, research and development, quality control, quality assurance, planning, warehouses, regulatory affairs, sales, marketing and many other capacities thereby leading to an improved overall performance and quality.

Dow College of biotechnology
Introduction



The Dow College of biotechnology is a constituent College of Dow University of Health Sciences. The college is located in the graceful building of Dow International Medical College, OJHA Campus. Currently, a four-year BS Biotechnology program is offered through Dow College of Biotechnology. The College has an excellent teaching faculty, comprising of foreign and nationally qualified PhD. scientists, who are extensively involved in research activities and hence are well aware of the recent developments in the field of sciences. Teaching methodology at Dow College of biotechnology is a well-planned blend of theory and practical skills learned in laboratories; therefore, this program is for students who have a passion for becoming scientists. For those students, Dow College of Biotechnology can provide an excellent platform.

The college offers the students diverse experiences and expertise, comprising of a quality educational environment that emphasizes learning through integration of theory with practical laboratory and field studies. Our program offers the students the opportunity to work on research projects that provide valuable insight and impact people's lives

The School of Public Health (SPH)
Introduction



The School of Public Health (SPH) was established in the year 2012. The formal academic teaching and activities started in the year 2013. The SPH’s academic activity involves not only the basic public health disciplines of biostatistics and epidemiology, but also include social sciences, health policy and management, and nutritional sciences. The first ever Undergraduate Program in Public Health Nutrition was also started by SPH.

Our programs are developed and taught by leading academics and practitioners addressing the major contemporary public health challenges. Researchers, academics and graduates of School of Public Health are expected to play a vital role in shaping the future of healthcare in Pakistan.

The main public health programs of the SPH include:

. Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) in Public Health.

. Masters of Public Health.

. Masters of Science in Health policy and Management.

. Masters of Science in Social and Behavioral Sciences.

. Masters of Science in Public Health Nutrition.

. Masters of Science in Biostatistics and Epidemiology.

. Four year Bachelors of Science in Public Health Nutrition.
