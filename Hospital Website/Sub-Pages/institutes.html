OJHA Institute Of Chest Diseases (OICD)
Historical Overview Of OJHA Institute
1942 Mr. Deep Chand Ojha donated 120 acres of land for the TB sanatorium, which was established in the form of small secluded huts for the patients of MDR-TB
1942 Mr. Deep Chand Ojha donated 120 acres of land for the TB sanatorium, which was established in the form of small secluded huts for the patients of MDR-TB 1965 The Main Building of Hospital was constructed with full facility and the bed strength of the Hospital was increased from 130 to 350.
1970 The land was handed over to the Government of Sindh. 1973 It was upgraded from Sanatorium to OJHA Institute of Chest Diseases.
1995 At OICD, Directly Observed Treatment (DOTs) strategy was implemented. 1996 Drug Resistant – Tuberculosis (DR-TB) treatment started at OICD.
2003 OICD becomes the constituent part of Dow University of Health Sciences. 2010 OJHA was selected as a Programmatic Management of Drug-resistant TB (PMDT) site of National Tuberculosis Program (NTP) for DR-TB management.
2013 Drug Resistant- TB unit notified. 2015 Treatment of Mycobacteria other than Mycobacterium Tuberculosis (MOTT) was started.
2016 Onwards OJHA Institute of Chest Diseases serves as center of excellence for Tuberculosis all over Pakistan.
Institute of Behavioral Sciences
Introduction Of Institute of Behavioral Sciences
The Institute of Behavioral Sciences is now a part of Dow University of Health Sciences, as part of the Department of Psychiatry, Dow International Medical College, DUHS (OJHA Campus). The institute aims to flourish and stand among the best mental health service providers of Pakistan.

This Institution offers consultation for psychiatric patients, drug addicts and provides medication to out-patients, as well as in-patients. For more than a decade, the institute has been contributing its share in serving humanity, under the supervision of well-known, qualified and experienced professionals, psychiatrists, psychologists and occupational therapists.

Currently, the institute is providing out-patient services through professional psychiatrists and psychologists. Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan Institute of Behavioral Sciences has a fully furnished In-patient department for psychiatric and drug patients, along with VIP Rooms, Private Rooms, Semi-Private Rooms and General Wards, separate for male and female patients. For the comfort and ease of our patients, there is also a fully temperature controlled psychiatric pharmacy that dispenses medications to in-patients and out-patients.

The Institute of Behavioral Sciences has sufficient staff, including 5 deputations from DUHS till date. Our staff members include an Executive Director, Faculty, SMOs, MOs, HOs, PG Trainees, Clinical Psychologists, Occupational Therapists, Admin Manager, Manager Finance, Accounts Officer, Q.A/Marketing Manager, IT Officer, Manager HIMS, HIMS Officer, Asst. Manager HR, HR Coordinator, Welfare Officer, Paramedic Staff (Registered Nurses & Aid Nurses), Porters, Office Attendants and Housekeeping Staff in all shifts i.e. morning, evening and night.
Dr. Ishrat-ul-Ebad Khan Institute of Oral Health
Introduction Of Dr. Ishrat-ul-Ebad Khan Institute of Oral Health
Dr. Ishrat-ul-Ebad Khan Institute of Oral Health Sciences (DIKIOHS) was established in the year 2006, as per vision of the honorable Vice Chancellor for the enhancement of academic excellence, increase of human resource and provision of excellent community health care. DIKIOHS is the most modern Institute of Pakistan that is fully equipped with the latest equipment’s and health care facilities and supervised by highly qualified dental surgeons that are scarcely found anywhere. This tremendous growth of the Institute proves that it will flourish in future at a very brisk rate to establish its fame in research, academics and community health care delivery system so that it can be at the top rating list of universities on the globe.

Dr. Ishrat-Ul-Ebad Khan Institute of Oral Health Sciences (DIKIOHS) currently has:

. The Dental Hospital for public.
. The Postgraduate Training and Research Center.
. School of Dental Auxiliary Sciences.
Dow Institute of Radiology
Introduction Of Dow Institute of Radiology
The Dow Institute of Radiology was established in 2008 to have the highest quality medical imaging, research opportunities, and Radiology educational experiences. The diagnostic modalities installed at Dow-Rad, are providing prompt and accurate diagnosis of different diseases at highly competitive rates which has made use of imaging modalities possible by general public. Besides providing economic benefits to the patients, the department of Imaging Services is committed to be at the forefront of new developments and clinical applications in the rapidly changing field of medical imaging.
National Institute of Liver and Gastro Intestinal Diseases
Introduction Of National Institute of Liver and Gastro Intestinal Diseases
This Institute was established in May 2004, initially on the first floor of Medical Unit V of Civil Hospital Karachi as the Sarwar Zuberi Liver Center in the name of a hepatologist, Madam Sarwar Jahan Zuberi. This center worked for ten years and provided services to about more than 100,000 patients. A few years later, in 2008, considering the load of Liver diseases in Pakistan and especially the Province of Sindh, a National Institute of Liver and Gastro Intestinal Diseases (NILGID) was established at OJHA Campus of Dow University.

This institution has a dedicated unit that can take care of patients suffering from liver and gastrointestinal diseases and also provides state of the art health facilities to patients with specific liver diseases with respect and dignity, through multidisciplinary approaches, in addition to providing research and other academic activities.
Institute of Bio Medical Sciences
Introduction Of Institute of Bio Medical Sciences
Institute of Bio Medical Sciences has been established for MPhil and PhD students who are interested in the key areas of Bio Medical Sciences. The Institute has developed the modular format to allow a greater range of options within the program to meet the requirements of medically trained personnel who wish to pursue a teaching and research career in the medical and allied health sciences.

The programs offered provide a sound understanding of human health and diseases and diagnostic approaches at the molecular level, applying contemporary techniques of modern biomedicine. It provides opportunities to acquire and develop the skills of synthesis, analysis and decision making, required for careers in the field of medical research. Thus, the students are trained as subject specialists, as researchers and resource persons, and well equipped with the methodologies of modern trends of teaching and research in the basic medical sciences. The program involves both course work and an original research thesis component.
